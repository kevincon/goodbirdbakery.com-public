Meet the Baker

Hi, I'm Kacha!

Baking has been part of my life for as long as I can remember.

I loved watching my mom bake apple pies every fall (and always hoped to snag a piece of apple). I started baking as a teenager and soon took over making cookies and cakes for our family's celebrations.

My love of baking blossomed into a passionate hobby after college.

I studied music at San Francisco State University and worked as a barista and an office assistant after graduating. The COVID-19 pandemic led me to become a homemaker, and baking became a way for me to share some joy with family, friends, and local businesses in a difficult time.

Positive feedback from the community encouraged me to start selling my baked goods.

I have been overwhelmed by the support from everyone who has tried my baked goods. Their interest inspired me to start this cottage bakery business.

Outside of baking...

In my spare time, I volunteer at the animal shelter, practice water coloring, and watch birds with my husband. I especially enjoy seeing our local yellow-billed magpies!

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a real bakery?

Good Bird Bakery is a cottage micro bakery in Fair Oaks, California operating under a Class A cottage food operation permit (#PR158343-A) issued by the Sacramento County Environmental Management Department. All products are made in a home kitchen.

Do you deliver?

At this time we only offer in-person pickup around Fair Oaks, California.

How often do products become available?

We typically offer products for order about once a month.

Why are you called "Good Bird Bakery"?

My husband and I enjoy bird watching, and the name was inspired by our admiration for the yellow-billed magpie:



This curious corvid is only found in one place in the entire world: where we live in California's Central Valley.

Magpies are often misunderstood for being mischevious or even aggressive birds, but they're actually extremely intelligent, social, and protective.

We think the yellow-billed magpie is great, and we often tell each other, "be a good bird..." like the yellow-billed magpie! ‚ò∫Ô∏è
